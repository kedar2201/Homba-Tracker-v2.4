Scoring Parameters Guide

Welcome to the documentation for the tracking radar's Buy Opportunity Engine. The engine uses a highly customizable system to grade incoming scrips based on Technical Strength, Valuation, and Business Quality.

⚖️ Custom Weights Geometry

You can completely redefine what the engine looks for. If you don't care about a parameter (like RSI), you can drag its slider down to 0 PTS. By setting a slider to 0, you tell the backend to completely remove that check from its mathematical grading for that stock.

You can then freely allocate those points to other rules. If you only care about Moving Averages, you can assign 100 points to DMA and 0 to everything else! The engine auto-normalizes the total back to 100%.

🚀 The 'Either/Or' Technical Philosophy

In V2.0, the engine uses Either/Or Pathway Scoring for the technical side to avoid contradicting itself (punishing a breakout stock because it isn't "dipping"):

  • Reversion Logic (Buy the Dip): Grades the stock strictly on how deeply it has pulled back, how low its RSI is, and whether it is touching a Moving Average floor.
  • Momentum Logic (Ride the Wave): Grades the stock strictly on whether it is breaking a 3-month high on massive volume spikes.

The engine calculates both paths independently and automatically uses whichever path scores higher.

📊 Parameters Breakdown

1. Dip Depth

  • What it checks: How far the current price has dropped from its recent 30-day high.
  • Impact: Increasing this weight tells the engine that buying "cheap" rel. to recent history is paramount.
  • Ideal Config: An 8% target dip is a healthy standard pullback. Use 12-15% for highly volatile small caps.

2. RSI Logic

  • What it checks: The Relative Strength Index (RSI). Values under 30 are statistically oversold.
  • Impact: Emphasizing this means you only want to buy when the herd is aggressively selling.
  • Ideal Config: A threshold around 38-40 is an ideal "floor" catch for a quality company without waiting for an absolute historic crash.

3. DMA Proximity Rules

  • What it checks: Is the stock bouncing exactly on its 50-Day or 200-Day Moving Averages?
  • Impact: Very high weights here turn the engine into an exclusive "Moving Average bounce" tracker.
  • Ideal Config: Proximity within 2-3% of the 50 DMA is an excellent technical entry zone.

4. Breakout + Volume

  • What it checks: Does the stock crack its 3-month high while trading volume is at least 80% higher than average?
  • Impact: Crucial for momentum. A breakout without volume is often a trap; this guards against that.

5. Market Bonus Filter

  • What it checks: Evaluates whether the broader NIFTY 50 trend is positive.
  • Impact: If you only want to buy stocks when the broader economy is in a proven bull run, increase this.

6. P/E Discount

  • What it checks: Compares the stock's current Price-to-Earnings ratio to its historical 5-Year Avg PE.
  • Impact: A high weight forces patience, as stocks rarely trade below 5-yr averages unless there is panic.

7. PEG Edge

  • What it checks: Price/Earnings to Growth. A PEG < 1 means it is undervalued relative to growth.
  • Impact: Penalizes highly hyped stocks where earnings aren't keeping up with the share price rally.

8. ROE Quality Check

  • What it checks: Return on Equity. How much profit does the company generate with shareholder money?
  • Impact: Ensures you don't buy "trash" just because it dipped. A high weight acts as a strict firewall.
  • Ideal Config: ROE > 15-18% is an excellent standard.

9. Sector Specifics (ROCE / NIM / EV)

  • What it checks: Because banks operate differently than manufacturing, this dynamically switches metrics based on the sector you chose!
    • Standard: Return on Capital Employed (ROCE).
    • Banking: Net Interest Margin (NIM) & Gross Non-Performing Assets (GNPA).
    • Insurance: Embedded Value (EV) Growth & Solvency Ratios.
